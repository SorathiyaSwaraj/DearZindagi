How does cryptocurrency work?

Cryptocurrencies operate on a technology called blockchain, which is a decentralized and distributed ledger that records all transactions across a network of computers. When someone initiates a cryptocurrency transaction, it is broadcasted to a network of decentralized nodes for verification. These nodes use complex mathematical algorithms to validate and authenticate the transaction. Once verified, the transaction is added to a block along with other transactions, forming a chain of blocks, hence the term blockchain. This process ensures transparency, security, and immutability of the transaction record. Additionally, cryptocurrencies utilize cryptographic techniques to secure transactions and control the creation of new units. For example, Bitcoin uses a process called mining, where miners solve complex mathematical puzzles to validate transactions and add them to the blockchain. In return for their efforts, miners are rewarded with newly created bitcoins. Overall, cryptocurrencies facilitate peer-to-peer transactions without the need for intermediaries, offering greater financial autonomy and security to users.
